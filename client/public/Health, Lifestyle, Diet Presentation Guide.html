Slide-by-slide layout (top-to-bottom)

  1. Title Slide

  • Subtitle: "Importance of Healthy Living" under the title.

  • Visual: Simple health-related icon or small photo (e.g., person jogging or food plate) on the right.
  1. Introduction to Health
  • Heading: "What is Health?"

  • Why it matters: 2 bullets with everyday examples (e.g., "Good health helps you study and play sports").
  • Visual: Small infographic or three-icon row representing the three dimensions.
  1. Dimensions of Health
  • Heading: "Dimensions of Health"
  • Bulleted list: Physical Health, Mental & Emotional Health, Social Health, Spiritual & Environmental Health.
  • For each, add a 1-line plain-language explanation and a small icon.
  1. Physical Health
  • Heading: "Physical Health"
  • Bullets: Role of exercise, balanced diet, sleep (one short tip each).
  • Warning: One bullet on avoiding harmful habits (smoking, excessive alcohol).
  • Real-life tip: Example: "Walk 30 minutes a day — easy way to start."
  • Visual: Photo or icon of exercise + small checklist.
  1. Mental & Emotional Health
  • Heading: "Mental & Emotional Health"
  • Bullets: Stress management, emotional balance, resilience with one practical technique each (deep breathing, journaling, talking to a friend).
  • Example: Short student-focused example (exam stress tip).
  • Visual: Calm/brain icon and a short 2-step stress-relief suggestion box.
  1. Social Health
  • Heading: "Social Health"
  • Bullets: Importance of relationships, family & friends, community interaction.
  • Practical tip: Join a club or volunteer once a month.
  • Visual: Group icon or photo with 2 short example activities.
  1. Concepts of Health
  • Heading: "Concepts of Health"
  • Bullets (each 1 line): Biomedical Concept (disease-focused), Ecological Concept (environmental influence), Psychosocial Concept (mind & society), Holistic Concept (whole-person view).
  • Visual: 2x2 grid of short labels + icons.
  1. Holistic Concept of Health
  • Heading: "Holistic Concept"
  • Bullets: Explain influence of social, economic, political, and environmental factors (one short line each).
  • Quote/explanation: “Sound mind in a sound body” — 1–2 lines explaining the phrase in simple terms.
  • Visual: Venn-style illustration (health overlap) or layered circles.
  1. New Philosophy of Health
  • Heading: "New Philosophy of Health"
  • Bullets: Health as a human right, link between health and development, social responsibility (each 1 short line).
  • Real-life example: Community vaccination drive or public park promoting exercise.
  • Visual: Small icon set (scales/rights, growth arrow, community).
  1. Lifestyle and Health
  • Heading: "Lifestyle and Health"
  • Bullets: Importance of daily habits for preventing disease; how choices add up over time.
  • Practical tip: Replace one unhealthy habit per month (example: swap soda for water).
  • Visual: Before/after small comparison graphic.
  1. Key Lifestyle Habits
  • Heading: "Key Habits for Healthy Living"
  • Bulleted checklist: Exercise regularly, Don’t smoke, Manage stress, Sleep well, Regular health checkups.

  • Visual: Checklist icons with short tick marks.
  1. Healthy Diet

  1. Conclusion
  • Heading: "Conclusion"
  • Bullets: Short summary of key points (health dimensions, lifestyle habits, healthy diet).
  • Closing message (large, bold): "Healthy lifestyle = Healthy life".

  • Visual: Positive image (smiling group or healthy food arrangement).

  • Color theme: Calming green and soft blue palette (use green for action items/tips, blue for headings/background accents).
  • Typography: Clean sans-serif (title larger, body 24–28pt for student readability).
  • Layout: Generous white space, clear headings, bullet-based content, left-aligned text.
  • Icons & Images: Use simple flat icons for each slide's main points and 4–5 photos (diverse people, food, exercise) spread across slides.
  • Charts/Graphics: Use a simple plate/pyramid for food groups and a small 2-column table for the meal plan.
  • Tone & Language: Short sentences, student-friendly vocabulary, active voice.
  • Accessibility tips: Use high contrast text on backgrounds and large bullets for readability.

Notes for the designer

  • Keep each slide to 3–6 bullets or 1 short table/graphic for clarity.
  • Use placeholders for Name / Class / Date on the title slide so they can be filled in easily.
  • Add small icons next to headings for visual interest and quicker scanning.
